What I built

Ava is a governed AI claims agent, presented for a fictional insurer, Albion Mutual. She handles a claim end to end: conversational intake, AI triage, fraud and vulnerability checks, deterministic routing, and human approval on anything sensitive. Around the agent I built a full web application with a customer portal, an admin dashboard, a multi team console, a live database view and an observability page, all in the Jeen brand.

Worth flagging clearly: I already had n8n self hosted, so rather than spin up a trial environment, I built the workflow directly on my own infrastructure, the NAVADA Edge network. On top of that I built the web application itself, Ava and every page you will see. The result is one connected system running on real, owned infrastructure.

How it works, and how data flows

A claim moves through six governed stages. The key design principle is that the model assists but never acts. It produces a constrained, auditable assessment, and a separate deterministic rule, plus a person, decides every outcome.

1 Intake. The first notice of loss arrives at a single n8n webhook, whether the customer talks to Ava or submits a form. One governed entry point for every channel.
2 AI triage. An OpenAI model reads the claim and returns strict JSON: claim type, severity, a fraud risk score from 0 to 100 with reasons, an FCA vulnerability flag, a routing recommendation and an adjuster summary. This is the only place the model forms a judgement, and it is bound to a schema.
3 System of record. The claim and the full assessment are written to PostgreSQL with a generated claim reference, creating a defensible audit trail from the first second.
4 Decision engine. A deterministic rule turns the assessment into accept, refer or decline, and selects the team. Claims at or above five thousand pounds, high severity, or involving a vulnerable customer go to the Adjuster. Fraud at or above 60 goes to Legal. Everything else is accepted to Finance. The logic is explicit and inspectable, never buried in a prompt.
5 Human in the loop. Referred claims land in the assigned team's queue. Ava emails the team, and they approve, decline, request more information or escalate through a shared console. No high value, high risk or vulnerable customer claim is ever actioned without a named person.
6 Notify and close the loop. Ava sends the claimant a branded acknowledgement and an update at every later stage, and alerts the team with links straight to the console and the exact workflow execution.

Where the data is stored

PostgreSQL 17 is the system of record. Every claim, every assessment and every team action, automated or human, is timestamped and retained, which gives us a replayable audit trail.

Secrets stay server side. API keys and database credentials live only inside the encrypted credential store in n8n. The browser only ever sees the claim form and the result, never a key.

Grafana and Prometheus provide observability. The workflow exposes metrics that feed a live dashboard, so the system can be watched in production.

Governed access. Hosting runs through a Cloudflare tunnel over an encrypted Tailscale mesh, with no inbound ports exposed on the host and traffic terminated at the edge.
